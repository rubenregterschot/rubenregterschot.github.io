Welcome!

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Rehabilitation & BioMechanics in the Department of Rehabilitation Medicine at the Erasmus Medical Center (the Netherlands). Before this, I was a PhD candidate and Researcher at the University Medical Center Groningen and a Research Scientist at Philips Research.

Currently my research is mainly focused on the development of a feedback intervention aimed at improving arm-hand usage during daily life in stroke survivors. Objective feedback on arm-hand movements will be given based on measurements with body-fixed motion sensors. At the same time I work on a variety of problems in BioMechanics and Movement Analysis.

I am the developer of an innovative, practical and clinically valid sensor-based method for the assessment of mobility in older adults. [PhD dissertation] [press release]

I also developed a new physical therapy based on whole body vibration for the enhancement of cognitive functioning in healthy individuals, (immobile) older adults and ADHD patients. [Publication in PLOS ONE] [press release in GEO magazine (Russia)]

Furthermore, on this website I provide Lecture Notes on BioMechanics for beginning graduate students. The Lecture Notes contain a fairly high level of mathematical detail compared to commonly used text books. Topics include: functions & sets, tensor algebra, calculus (including differential equations), translations & rotations, Newtonian mechanics, multi-joint kinematics & dynamics, control theory, balance & balance control.
