Welcome!

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Rehabilitation Medicine at the Erasmus Medical Center (the Netherlands).

Currently my research is mainly focused on the development of a new intervention that stimulates people with a stroke to use the arm outside therapy more frequently and intensively in order to improve arm-hand functioning. The main part of the intervention is an innovative hand activity tracker that will be developed to measure arm use objectively and provide patient-specific feedback to stimulate upper limb use. At the same time I work on several other research projects. More information about my current research is available on the Current Research page (see above).

I am the developer of an innovative, practical and clinically valid sensor-based method for the assessment of mobility in older adults. [PhD dissertation] [press release]

I also developed a new physical therapy based on whole body vibration for the enhancement of cognitive functioning in healthy individuals, (immobile) older adults and ADHD patients. [Publication in PLOS ONE] [press release in GEO magazine (Russia)]
