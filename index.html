Welcome!

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Rehabilitation Medicine at the Erasmus Medical Center (the Netherlands).

My research interest is in 1) the development of algorithms for the analysis of wearable sensor data, and 2) the development of wearable sensor systems that apply objective feedback to stimulate and improve movement in clinical populations (e.g. stroke). My research is on the border between data science, human movement science and rehabilitation.

Currently my research is mainly focused on 1) the development of algorithms for the analysis of wrist-worn sensor data, and 2) the development of a new intervention that stimulates people with a stroke to use the arm outside therapy more frequently and intensively in order to improve arm-hand functioning. The main part of the intervention is an innovative Arm Activity Tracker that will be developed to measure arm use objectively and provide personalized feedback to stimulate arm use. At the same time I work on several other research projects.

I am the developer of an innovative, practical and clinically valid sensor-based method for the assessment of mobility in older adults. [LINK]

I also developed a new physical therapy based on whole body vibration for the enhancement of cognitive functioning in healthy individuals, (immobile) older adults and ADHD patients. [LINK]
